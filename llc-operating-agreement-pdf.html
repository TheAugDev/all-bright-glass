ARTICLE IV: COMPENSATION & FINDER'S FEES

4.1. Management Compensation: Members shall not receive salaries for their management roles. Compensation is provided exclusively through the profit-sharing distributions.

4.2. Fieldwork Compensation: When Arnel Oco performs direct technical labor, he shall be compensated separately as an independent contractor. This is a Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) and is paid before the calculation of net profit.

4.3. Tiered Finder's Fee Program:

  • Fee Structure: A fee will be paid for successful new client referrals based on the total contract value:
    • 5% for contracts up to $25,000.
    • 8% for contracts from $25,001 to $75,000.
    • 10% for contracts over $75,000.
  • Public Referrals: A one-time fee, based on the structure above, is paid to any non-Member for the initial contract signed by a referred client.
  • Internal Referrals (Lifetime Commission): A lifetime commission, based on the structure above, is paid to any Member (except the Sales Lead) for the initial contract and all subsequent business from that same referred client.
  • Ineligibility: Jun Doydora Jr. is not eligible to receive finder's fees, as this is a primary function of his role compensated via profit share.

ARTICLE VI: TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

6.2. Right of First Refusal: In the event a Member receives a bona fide offer from a third party to purchase their interest, the Selling Member must first offer their interest to the other remaining Members on the same terms before selling to the third party.

ARTICLE VII: DISSOCIATION OF A MEMBER

A Member shall cease to be a member upon voluntary withdrawal, death, incapacitation, or removal by a unanimous vote of the other Members for a material breach of this Agreement.

ARTICLE VIII: BUYOUT PROVISIONS

Upon a Member's dissociation, the Company and/or Remaining Members have the option to purchase the interest. The value will be determined by mutual agreement or, failing that, by an independent appraiser. Payment can be made via a lump sum or a promissory note over a period not to exceed five (5) years.

ARTICLE IX: DISSOLUTION OF THE COMPANY

The Company shall be dissolved by a unanimous written agreement of all Members, the sale of all assets, or a judicial decree. Upon dissolution, assets will be used to pay creditors, then liabilities to Members, with the remainder distributed to Members according to their ownership interest.
